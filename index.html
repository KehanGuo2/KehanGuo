I am a Ph.D. candidate in Computer Science at the University of Notre Dame, advised by Prof. Xiangliang Zhang. This summer I am a research intern at Apple, working on reinforcement-learning post-training for large language models; previously I conducted research at Amazon AWS AI and ByteDance.

I work on RL post-training and reward design for LLM agents, organized around one question: when a model is optimized against a proxy — a reward model, a verifier, a frozen environment — where does the optimization pressure actually go? I pursue it on two fronts — reward-aligned generation, treating the noise–data coupling in flow matching as a free interface for property control, and the environment side of agentic RL, where verifiers, tools, and corpora shape (and are exploited by) the policies trained against them — with scientific reasoning as a testbed for verifiable ground truth and unforgiving failure modes.
